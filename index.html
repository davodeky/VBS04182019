Sunday's Sermon

Bible Reading

Theme: I have got to get myself together
  • Matthew 21:1-17

Sunday's Sermon
  • As Jesus entered Jerusalem, He was welcomed by people on the streets
  • The same should have been expected of His entry into the temple
  • However, in the temple, He finds people minding their own business and not the business of the Lord.
  • We shouldn’t be like the people in Jerusalem; making noise outside, but having a disconnect in the house of the Lord.
  • The house of the Lord needs to be a place of prayer.
  • A place where: if we are lost out there we come to be found; if we lose our identity out there we come to find our identity in church; not a place where we come to get our moment of faith; not a place of criticizing.
  • Jesus is not the son of God, just to be shouted about and Him not being a reflection of what we are in the inside.
  • If we come to the house of God and create cliques, we have missed the point.
  • We are all children of God and we should see God in each of us.
  • If God has to be praised, He has to be praised for real.
  • May our attitudes, our gatherings praise Jesus.
  • The church is a crossroad of divinity and humanity.
  • We are all children of God, we are all important to God and it is our responsibility to fix the house of God.
  • Do we worship the Lord only with our lips and not in our inside?
  • We don’t have to be everybody’s best friend, but we should love everyone with the love of God.

Disciplines For The Disciple

Disciplines of a Disciple
We have thus far looked at the disciplines of:

  • Prayer- secret, simple, and steadfast.
  • Meditation- contemplating God, His works, His words, and things worthy of virtue.
  • Fasting- humbling oneself before God when joined with sincere prayer.
  • Singing- which edifies the singer as well as praises God.
  • Fellowship- communing with other Christians as we engage in spiritual activities.
  • Giving- sharing sacrificially of money, time, and ability as God's grace allows.
  • Teaching- first become a student, followed by a doer and then a teacher.

Questions
  • What discipline would you like focus on improving this season?

The Crucifixion of Jesus Christ
